## 🎮 What are the **Four Pillars** in Game Design (GCD)?

The **Four Pillars** are **4 short, powerful concepts** that describe the *core identity* of your game.
They act like the **foundation** of the game: everything you design later should respect them.

Think of them as the **DNA of your game**.

If someone reads only these four pillars, they should instantly understand:

* What the game *feels like*
* What makes it *special*
* What type of experience the player should expect

They help the team stay aligned, especially in fast prototyping (like during a hackathon).

---

## 🧱 Why Four Pillars?

Because beginners tend to create huge game ideas.
The four pillars force you to:

* keep it **focused**
* avoid feature creep
* build a consistent prototype
* communicate quickly and clearly

In a hackathon, if you don’t have your four pillars, the team won’t know the direction.

---

## 🎯 What a Pillar Looks Like

A “pillar” is usually **one or two words**, very strong, very clear.

Examples:

* **Speed**
* **Exploration**
* **Cooperation**
* **Tactics**
* **Survival**
* **Humor**
* **Chaos**
* **Precision**
* **Narrative Emotion**

Each pillar describes one essential aspect of the game experience.

---

## 🔥 Example: Mario (simplified)

Mario’s pillars could be:

1. **Jumping** – the core mechanic
2. **Exploration** – discover levels
3. **Timing** – obstacles and enemies
4. **Progression** – reach the flag

Everything in Mario reinforces these.

---

## ⚔️ Example: A Hackathon Roguelike Shooter

1. **Speed** – fast gameplay
2. **Chaos** – enemies everywhere
3. **Replayability** – random levels
4. **Power Fantasy** – crazy weapons

If someone proposes an idea that does not fit the pillars → you reject it.

---

## 🧩 How to Define Your Own Four Pillars

1. Ask: **What should the player feel?**
2. Ask: **What is fun in our game?**
3. Ask: **What makes our game different?**
4. Pick the **4 strongest ideas**.

They must be:

* Short
* Clear
* Memorable
* Core to the gameplay
* Easy to explain to anyone

---

## 🏁 In short:

**The Four Pillars = the 4 strongest concepts that define your game’s experience.**

They guide your design and help you stay focused, especially when building a prototype under time pressure.
